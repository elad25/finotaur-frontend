## In plain words

The forex market runs 24 hours a day, five days a week — but not all hours are equal. It is divided into four overlapping **sessions**: Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York. The busiest trading activity clusters around **session overlaps**, when two major financial centers are open simultaneously and liquidity peaks.

## Quick demo

The London–New York overlap runs from roughly 8 AM to 12 PM Eastern Time. During this four-hour window on a typical weekday, EUR/USD can move 60–100 pips — double or triple what it moves during the quieter Asian session overnight. A trader who only watches the screen at 2 AM Eastern is grinding through the slowest, choppiest stretch of the day.

## Full explanation

### The four sessions

| Session | Approximate UTC hours | Key pairs |
|---|---|---|
| Sydney | 22:00 – 07:00 | AUD/USD, NZD/USD |
| Tokyo | 00:00 – 09:00 | USD/JPY, AUD/JPY |
| London | 07:00 – 16:00 | EUR/USD, GBP/USD, EUR/GBP |
| New York | 12:00 – 21:00 | USD majors, USD/CAD |

Times shift by one hour during daylight saving transitions, which don't occur on the same dates globally — a minor nuisance worth noting.

### Why sessions matter

Volume and volatility are not uniform. Liquidity determines:

- **How tight spreads are** — brokers widen spreads in thin markets, increasing your cost.
- **How smoothly orders fill** — thin liquidity means slippage is more likely.
- **How far price moves** — big moves need big participation.

The **London open** (07:00 UTC) is often called the most important moment of the forex day. European institutional players enter the market, driving sharp directional moves that can establish the day's range.

The **London–New York overlap** (12:00–16:00 UTC) is typically the highest-volume window for majors like EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Major economic data from both regions hits during this window, and two enormous trading centers are active simultaneously.

The **Tokyo session** matters most for yen pairs and commodity-linked currencies (AUD, NZD). If news out of Japan or China breaks, this is when it gets priced in.

The **Sunday open** (Sydney) is a separate risk moment: the market reopens after the weekend and can gap if significant news broke while it was closed.

### Quiet hours — when to be careful

- **Asian session mid-hours** (02:00–05:00 UTC): thin volume, wide spreads on non-Asian pairs, choppy price action.
- **Pre-London lull** (05:00–07:00 UTC): transitional; pairs like EUR/USD can drift without direction.
- **End of New York** (19:00–22:00 UTC): volume drops sharply; spreads widen again.

### Session overlaps at a glance

- Tokyo–London overlap (~07:00–09:00 UTC): brief but active for EUR/JPY.
- London–New York overlap (~12:00–16:00 UTC): highest-volume window globally.

### Why it matters for a trader

Knowing session structure lets you match your strategy to the environment. Range strategies can work in quiet Asian hours; breakout and momentum strategies are better suited to the London open or the overlap. Trading during low-volume windows with wide spreads silently erodes your edge — you pay more to enter and get worse fills on exit. Scheduling your trading hours deliberately is one of the simplest and most overlooked edges in forex.
