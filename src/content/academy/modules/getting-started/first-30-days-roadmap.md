## In plain words

Your first 30 days as a trader should be about building **habits and knowledge**, not chasing profits. The traders who survive long-term are rarely the ones who made money in week one — they are the ones who built a repeatable process before risk got real. This roadmap gives you a concrete, week-by-week structure so you are not guessing what to focus on.

## Quick demo

Two beginners start on the same day. Beginner A jumps straight into live trading with $5,000, acts on tips, sizes up when winning, and blows up in week two. Beginner B spends weeks one and two on paper trading, builds a written plan, logs every trade, and does not go live until week three with $1,000 at 0.5% risk per trade. After 30 days, Beginner A is out of the game. Beginner B has 40 logged trades, a 38% win rate with a 2:1 reward/risk ratio — and a foundation to improve from.

## Full explanation

### Week 1 — Set up and study

Before placing any trade, real or simulated:

- **Open your brokerage account** and familiarize yourself with the platform. Practice navigating charts, watchlists, and the order entry screen.
- **Activate paper trading.** Do not use real money yet.
- **Pick one market and one strategy to focus on.** The most common beginner mistake is learning ten things shallowly. Depth beats breadth. Pick stocks, futures, or forex — not all three.
- **Read the basics.** Work through the foundational modules in this academy: market structure, order types, risk management, and one technical or fundamental framework.
- **Set up your journal.** Create the spreadsheet or tool you will use from day one of paper trading.

### Week 2 — Paper trade with intention

- **Trade every day on paper**, but treat each trade as if it were real money. Enter size, stop levels, and target levels before you click.
- **Aim for 5–10 paper trades by end of week.** Log every one.
- **Review daily.** What worked? What did you break? Did you enter impulsively or wait for your criteria?
- **Refine your written plan.** After each day, update your rule set. Entry criteria, stop placement, position size formula, daily loss limit.

### Week 3 — Go live small

If your paper trading is consistent (you are following rules, not just getting lucky), transition to real money with these constraints:

- **Start with a small account size** — only what you can afford to lose entirely without financial hardship.
- **Risk 0.5%–1% per trade maximum.** On a $2,000 account, that is $10–20 per trade. This feels too small. That is the point.
- **Trade one instrument only.** Master it.
- **Continue journaling every trade.** Real money adds emotion; journaling keeps you honest.

### Week 4 — Review and adjust

- **Pull your journal data.** Calculate: win rate, average winner, average loser, expectancy. Expectancy = (Win Rate × Average Win) − (Loss Rate × Average Loss).
- **Identify your biggest recurring mistake.** Every trader has one at this stage — entering too early, holding losers too long, skipping stops. Name it.
- **Set one specific rule to address it.** Not five — one.
- **Decide on your next 30-day focus.** Are you ready to increase size slightly? Do you need more paper time? Is the strategy working or does it need revision?

### What success looks like after 30 days

Success at 30 days is not profitability — it is:
- A written trading plan that you actually follow.
- A journal with at least 30 logged trades.
- A clear understanding of your biggest weakness.
- Survival — account is intact or only modestly down.
- A specific plan for the next 30 days.

Profitable trading is a skill built over months and years. The traders who improve fastest are the ones who treat the early period as training, not as an opportunity to get rich quickly. Build the foundation and the results will follow.
