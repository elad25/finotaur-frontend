## In plain words
A **lot** (forex) or **contract** (futures/options) is the standardized unit of position size in a given market — the building block you use to define how large your trade is.

## Quick demo
In forex, one standard lot = 100,000 units of the base currency. Trading one lot of EUR/USD means controlling €100,000. One ES futures contract controls 50 × the S&P 500 index level — roughly $250,000 in notional value.

## Full explanation
Different markets use different units for position size:

**Forex lots:**
- **Standard lot** — 100,000 units of base currency
- **Mini lot** — 10,000 units (0.10 lots)
- **Micro lot** — 1,000 units (0.01 lots)

The pip value in dollars depends on lot size. On a standard EUR/USD lot, each pip is worth approximately $10.

**Futures contracts:**
Each futures product has a fixed contract specification. The contract size determines the dollar value of every tick movement. Traders cannot divide contracts — if you trade one ES contract, you control the full notional amount.

**Options contracts:**
In US equity options, one contract typically represents 100 shares of the underlying stock.

**Stocks:**
Shares are more flexible — you can buy 1 to any number. Position size is simply shares × price per share.

Understanding lot and contract size is essential for accurate position sizing and risk calculation. Always confirm the exact specifications before trading an unfamiliar instrument.
