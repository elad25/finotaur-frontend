## In plain words
**Support** is a price level where buying tends to emerge and halt a decline; **resistance** is a level where selling tends to emerge and halt an advance — they are the floors and ceilings of price action.

## Quick demo
A stock repeatedly bounces off $48 over three months — that $48 level is support. Every time it pushes toward $55, sellers overwhelm buyers and price pulls back — $55 is resistance. A decisive close above $55 would signal a breakout.

## Full explanation
Support and resistance levels form wherever significant buying or selling has occurred in the past, leaving a "memory" in the market that future participants reference.

Common sources of support and resistance:
- **Prior highs and lows** — levels where price previously reversed
- **Round numbers** — psychological levels like $50, $100, $1,000
- **Moving averages** — dynamic levels that shift with price (50-day, 200-day MA)
- **VWAP** — intraday fair value that often acts as support or resistance
- **Volume nodes** — areas of high historical trading activity in volume profiles
- **Fibonacci retracements** — mathematically derived levels traders watch widely

Key behavior to understand:
- **Role reversal** — a broken support level often becomes resistance, and vice versa. Once $48 is broken decisively to the downside, it frequently acts as overhead resistance on any bounce back toward it.
- **Confluence** — a level where multiple sources of support/resistance align is considered stronger than any single source.
- **The more tests, the weaker** — each touch of a level consumes the liquidity behind it. A level tested many times without breaking is not necessarily stronger; it may be running out of buyers or sellers to sustain it.

Support and resistance are not precise lines but zones. Price rarely reverses at an exact price to the cent — give levels some room.
