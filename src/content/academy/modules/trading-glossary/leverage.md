## In plain words
**Leverage** lets you control a position larger than your actual cash by borrowing capital from your broker — amplifying both gains and losses.

## Quick demo
With 10:1 leverage, a $5,000 account can control a $50,000 position. A 2% move in your favor earns $1,000 (20% on your capital); a 2% move against you costs $1,000 — the same 20% loss.

## Full explanation
Leverage is expressed as a ratio (2:1, 10:1, 100:1) or as a margin percentage (50%, 10%, 1%). Your broker provides the difference between what you deposit and what you control.

Common leverage levels by asset class:
- **Stocks (US)** — up to 4:1 intraday for pattern day traders; 2:1 overnight
- **Futures** — leverage is built into contract structure; ES futures control ~$250K notional with ~$12K in margin
- **Forex** — retail platforms often offer 50:1 to 500:1
- **Crypto** — perpetual exchanges commonly offer 10:1 to 100:1

The mechanics cut both ways. Leverage amplifies P&L relative to your deposited capital, not the overall position size. This means a relatively small adverse move can wipe out your margin entirely.

Leverage is a tool, not a strategy. Most experienced traders use far less leverage than their broker allows, treating it as a convenience for capital efficiency rather than a way to maximize exposure.
