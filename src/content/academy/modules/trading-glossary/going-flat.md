## In plain words
**Going flat** means closing all open positions so that you have zero exposure to the market — neither long nor short anything.

## Quick demo
A day trader who started the morning with three long positions and one short closes all four trades by 3:55 PM. At market close, they are flat — no overnight risk, no margin in use.

## Full explanation
Being flat is a deliberate state, not the default absence of action. Experienced traders often choose to go flat at specific moments:

- **End of trading session** — day traders typically go flat before the close to avoid overnight gap risk.
- **Before high-impact events** — earnings releases, Fed announcements, or macro data where direction is uncertain.
- **After a losing streak** — stepping away to reset emotionally and reassess.
- **When the edge is unclear** — no setup, no trade. Flat is a valid position.

Going flat has real value that is easy to underestimate:
- **Zero risk** — you cannot lose money you are not in.
- **Psychological reset** — exiting all positions removes the cognitive load of managing open risk.
- **Optionality** — you are free to enter the next high-conviction setup without existing positions pulling your judgment.

The phrase "when in doubt, get out" reflects a flat-first mindset. Knowing when not to trade is a skill in itself, and going flat is one of the clearest expressions of that discipline.
