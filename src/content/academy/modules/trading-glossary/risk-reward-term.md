## In plain words
**Risk/reward** is the ratio of how much you stand to lose on a trade versus how much you stand to gain — expressed as a ratio like 1:2 or 1:3.

## Quick demo
You enter a trade risking $100 (distance to your stop) to target a $250 gain. Your risk/reward ratio is 1:2.5 — for every dollar you risk, you aim to make $2.50.

## Full explanation
Risk/reward (often written R:R) is calculated at the time of trade entry, before you know the outcome:

**Risk** = entry price minus stop-loss price (for a long trade)
**Reward** = target price minus entry price (for a long trade)
**Ratio** = Reward ÷ Risk

Common benchmarks:
- **1:1** — break-even minimum; you need to win more than 50% to be profitable.
- **1:2** — widely used; you can be profitable winning only 34% of trades.
- **1:3** — used by many swing traders; profitable above 25% win rate.

The critical insight is that risk/reward does not work in isolation — it must be paired with win rate to determine true edge (this is expectancy). A 1:10 risk/reward setup is worthless if you only win 5% of the time.

However, a favorable risk/reward ratio gives you margin for error: you can be wrong more often than you are right and still make money, as long as your winners are meaningfully larger than your losers. This asymmetry is the foundation of most professional trading approaches.
