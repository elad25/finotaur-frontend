## In plain words
**Drawdown** is the decline in your account (or a portfolio) from its highest point to a subsequent low — the measure of how far you fell from the peak before recovering.

## Quick demo
Your account grows from $10,000 to $14,000, then falls to $11,200 before recovering. That $2,800 drop from the $14,000 peak is a 20% drawdown.

## Full explanation
Drawdown is one of the most important risk metrics in trading because it measures what you actually experience in real time — the pain of losing money you had already made.

Key drawdown metrics:

- **Maximum drawdown (MDD)** — the largest peak-to-trough decline over the full history of an account. A 50% MDD means the account was cut in half at its worst point, requiring a 100% gain just to return to the previous high.
- **Current drawdown** — how far below the peak you are right now.
- **Drawdown duration** — how long it took to recover to the previous peak.

Why drawdown matters:

The math of losses is asymmetric. A 20% loss requires a 25% gain to recover. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain. This asymmetry makes avoiding large drawdowns more valuable than chasing large gains.

For prop traders, drawdown is often the primary constraint — breach the firm's drawdown limit and your account is paused or closed. Monitoring your equity curve and drawdown in real time is a core discipline of professional trading.
