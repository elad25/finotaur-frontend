## In plain words

Most major futures contracts trade for nearly **23 hours a day**, five days a week — far longer than stock exchanges. This near-continuous market means futures can react to overnight news, foreign market moves, and early economic data releases in real time. At expiration, contracts settle either in **cash** (the more common outcome for financial futures) or via **physical delivery** of the actual commodity.

## Quick demo

ES futures open at 6:00 PM Eastern on Sunday and trade nearly without interruption until 5:00 PM Eastern on Friday. A major CPI report releases at 8:30 AM Eastern. ES reacts instantly — price moves 15 points in the minute after the release, before the US stock market opens at 9:30 AM. Traders who understood the futures session were positioned; those who only watch stocks woke up to a market that had already moved. That is the real-time advantage of futures.

## Full explanation

### CME Globex and the electronic session

Most futures trade on **CME Globex**, the electronic trading platform. The primary session for US index futures (ES, NQ, YM, RTY) runs:

- **Open**: 6:00 PM Eastern (Sunday through Thursday)
- **Close**: 5:00 PM Eastern (the following day)
- **Daily maintenance halt**: 5:00 PM to 6:00 PM Eastern (one hour)

This gives traders 23 hours of access per day. The **regular trading hours (RTH)** session — when the underlying stock market is open — runs 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM Eastern. Outside of RTH, you are in the **extended/electronic trading hours (ETH)** session, which is typically lower volume and wider spreads.

### Why the near-24-hour session matters

- **Overnight events**: earnings releases, geopolitical events, and foreign market moves during Asian or European sessions all show up in futures prices before US stocks open.
- **Economic data**: CPI, jobs reports, PPI, and Fed decisions often release before the US stock market opens. Futures absorb the impact in real time.
- **Sunday gap**: when US index futures re-open Sunday evening, any weekend news is immediately priced in. Monday's stock open often reflects a move that already happened Sunday night in futures.

### The pre-market as a guide

US traders watch ES and NQ futures before the market open to gauge sentiment. A futures market that is up 0.8% before the open signals a bullish open for stocks. This is not a perfect predictor of where stocks will close — intraday reversals happen constantly — but it is the most widely used pre-market signal.

### Settlement: cash vs. physical delivery

**Cash settlement** (financial futures):
At expiration, no assets change hands. Instead, the exchange calculates the **final settlement price** (typically an opening auction price on expiration day for equity index futures) and credits or debits the difference to each account. ES, NQ, and most interest rate futures are cash settled.

**Physical delivery** (most commodity futures):
At expiration, the seller delivers the actual commodity — crude oil barrels, gold, wheat — to the buyer at a designated location. Most traders and speculators never want physical delivery. They avoid it by closing or rolling the contract well before the **first notice day** (the date when delivery notices can begin) or the **last trading day**.

### Key dates to know

- **Last trading day**: the final day a contract can be traded.
- **First notice day** (FND): for physical delivery contracts, the first day the long (buyer) can be assigned delivery. Often several days before last trading day. Traders without storage or delivery infrastructure must close before FND.
- **Final settlement price**: the official price used to settle a cash-settled contract at expiration.

### Volatility patterns across the session

Not all hours are equally active. The highest volume and tightest spreads occur during:
1. The **US regular trading hours (9:30 AM – 4:00 PM ET)**
2. The **European open (3:00 AM – 5:00 AM ET)** for overlapping sessions
3. **Economic data releases** (typically 8:30 AM and 10:00 AM ET)

The **overnight session (8:00 PM – 7:00 AM ET)** is generally thin, with wider spreads and larger moves on less volume — not the ideal environment for many trading strategies.

### Why it matters for a trader

The 23-hour session is one of futures' most powerful advantages — and most underestimated risks. Positions held overnight are exposed to gaps at the re-open if a major event occurred during the maintenance hour or over a weekend. Knowing when your contract's highest-liquidity window is, and knowing the difference between RTH and ETH price behavior, helps you choose when to trade and when to stay flat.
