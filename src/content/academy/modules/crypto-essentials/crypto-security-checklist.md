## In plain words

Crypto security is not a one-time setup — it is a set of habits you maintain continuously. Because transactions are irreversible and there is no fraud department to call, **prevention is the only recovery**. A systematic checklist applied once and reviewed regularly eliminates the vast majority of the attack surface.

## Quick demo

After reading about a $2 million wallet hack caused by a SIM swap, Carlos spends 90 minutes locking down his setup: he moves his exchange 2FA from SMS to an authenticator app, enables a withdrawal address whitelist, generates a new seed phrase on a hardware wallet, and moves 80% of his holdings off the exchange. Six months later, his mobile carrier account is compromised — but his crypto is untouched because SMS authentication was already removed.

## Full explanation

### Exchange account security

- **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** — use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) or a hardware key (YubiKey). Never use SMS/text-message 2FA; SIM swaps are a common attack.
- **Use a unique, strong password** — at least 20 characters, generated by a password manager. Never reuse passwords.
- **Enable withdrawal address whitelisting** — restricts withdrawals to pre-approved addresses only. A hacker who logs in cannot send funds to their own wallet.
- **Enable email confirmation on withdrawals** — adds one more hurdle for unauthorized transfers.
- **Use a dedicated email address** for all crypto accounts — reduces exposure from data breaches of other services.

### Device and browser hygiene

- Keep your operating system and browser **up to date** — patches close vulnerabilities exploited in the wild.
- Use a **hardware wallet** for significant holdings — the single highest-impact security step.
- Do not install browser extensions you don't fully trust — malicious extensions can read your screen and intercept wallet interactions.
- Consider a **dedicated browser profile** for crypto transactions, kept separate from everyday browsing.
- Never perform crypto transactions on public Wi-Fi without a VPN.

### Wallet and key security

- **Store your seed phrase offline** — written on paper or stamped into metal. No photos, no cloud, no email.
- Keep **two physical copies** of your seed phrase in separate secure locations.
- Test your backup before relying on it: restore a fresh wallet from the seed phrase and verify the address matches.
- **Never share your seed phrase or private key** with anyone, ever — this is non-negotiable.
- Use a **separate wallet** for DeFi interactions (connecting to apps) versus long-term storage.

### Transaction hygiene

- **Always double-check the first 4 and last 4 characters** of any address you paste — malware can swap clipboard addresses.
- Send a small test transaction before large transfers.
- Verify the network (Ethereum vs. Polygon vs. Arbitrum, etc.) matches on both sides before sending.
- Revoke token approvals you no longer need — use tools like Revoke.cash to audit and remove old permissions you granted to apps.

### Ongoing habits

- Regularly audit which devices have access to your exchange account.
- Review your approved app connections on your wallets periodically.
- Stay skeptical of unsolicited messages on any platform — Discord, Telegram, Twitter/X.
- Do not announce your holdings publicly.

### If you suspect a compromise

- Move funds immediately to a fresh wallet created on a clean device.
- Change all passwords and 2FA from an uncompromised device.
- Contact the exchange to freeze your account if you believe it is accessed.

### Why it matters

Most crypto theft is not technically sophisticated — it exploits predictable human behavior: reused passwords, SMS 2FA, phishing clicks, and exposed seed phrases. Running through this checklist closes the door on the overwhelming majority of real-world attacks. Security is not the interesting part of crypto, but it is the prerequisite for everything else.
