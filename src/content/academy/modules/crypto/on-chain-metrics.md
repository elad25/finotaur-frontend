## In plain words

**On-chain metrics** are data points derived directly from a blockchain's public transaction history — things like how many addresses are active, how much is flowing into or out of exchanges, and how long coins have been held without moving. Because all this data is public and immutable, it provides a layer of market intelligence that doesn't exist in traditional finance. Analysts use it to gauge sentiment, supply pressure, and likely holder behavior.

## Quick demo

In late 2020, on-chain analysts noticed that a large volume of Bitcoin that had been dormant for years was moving to exchanges — a behavior associated with long-term holders preparing to sell. At the same time, exchange reserves started declining, meaning new buyers were withdrawing coins off exchanges into private wallets (a sign of holding conviction). These two signals pointed in opposite directions; monitoring which trend dominated helped frame the supply-demand picture without relying on social media sentiment.

## Full explanation

### Why on-chain data is unique

Every transaction on a public blockchain is recorded, timestamped, and auditable. This gives analysts a real view of flows that's impossible to get in traditional markets. You can see actual exchange inflows and outflows, coin age distribution, and miner behavior — not survey estimates, but raw facts about what participants are doing with real money.

### Key on-chain metrics

#### Active addresses
The number of unique addresses sending or receiving in a given period. A rising count suggests growing network usage; a decline can signal reduced interest. Compare to price to identify divergences.

#### Exchange inflows and outflows
- **Inflow** — coins moving from private wallets to exchange wallets. Often interpreted as preparation to sell.
- **Outflow** — coins moving from exchanges to private wallets. Often interpreted as buying and self-custodying ("hodling").
- **Exchange reserves** — the total balance held on exchanges. Declining reserves + rising price is generally a bullish supply signal.

#### HODL waves and coin age
**HODL waves** visualize what percentage of Bitcoin's supply last moved within a given timeframe. When the "older" bands (coins unmoved for 1–5 years) start shrinking, it means long-term holders are distributing. When they grow during bear markets, it suggests accumulation.

#### Realized price
The **realized price** is calculated by valuing each coin at the price it last moved, then averaging across all coins. It approximates the average cost basis of the market. When spot price falls below realized price, the average holder is at a loss — historically a condition associated with late-stage bear markets.

#### Miner metrics
- **Miner outflows** — miners sell regularly to cover operating costs (electricity, hardware). Spikes can add selling pressure.
- **Hash rate** — total computational power securing the network. Rising hash rate reflects miner confidence; a sharp drop may follow a price decline (miners capitulate and shut off unprofitable rigs).

#### Network Value to Transactions (NVT)
NVT compares market cap to on-chain transaction volume, analogous to a P/E ratio. High NVT suggests price is running ahead of fundamental on-chain activity. Low NVT suggests the network is being used extensively relative to its market cap.

#### Funding rates and open interest (futures)
On perpetual futures platforms, on-chain data reveals **funding rates** (whether longs or shorts are paying each other) and **open interest** (total leveraged positions outstanding). Rising open interest during a rally can signal over-leveraged longs vulnerable to a liquidation cascade.

### Limitations

- **Interpretation requires context** — most on-chain metrics are probabilistic signals, not certainties. Moving coins to an exchange doesn't guarantee a sale.
- **Exchange wallets are aggregated** — not all exchange movements are user-initiated; exchanges regularly consolidate wallets internally.
- **Layer 2 and off-chain activity is invisible** — transactions on Layer 2 networks or inside exchange matching engines don't appear on the Layer 1 chain.

### Why it matters for a trader

On-chain analysis adds a supply-side dimension to crypto trading that technical analysis alone cannot provide. Understanding exchange reserve trends, long-term holder behavior, and miner capitulation signals helps frame whether you're trading into an environment of genuine accumulation or distribution — context that chart patterns don't always reveal on their own.
