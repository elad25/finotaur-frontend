## In plain words

**Altcoins** are every cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. They range from large, established networks like Ethereum and Solana to thousands of smaller tokens that may have brief moments of intense attention before fading. Crypto markets are heavily driven by **narratives** — shared stories about which technology, sector, or use case is the next big thing — and capital tends to rotate rapidly from one narrative to the next.

## Quick demo

In early 2021, "DeFi summer" had already passed, and attention shifted to **NFTs**. Collections like CryptoPunks and Bored Apes attracted mainstream media coverage. Tokens associated with NFT marketplaces and gaming ("GameFi") surged hundreds of percent in weeks. By late 2021, that attention rotated into Layer 1 blockchains competing with Ethereum (Solana, Avalanche, Terra). Each narrative pulled billions of dollars in speculative capital, inflated quickly, then deflated — sometimes completely. A trader who identified and positioned early in each rotation made outsized returns; one who arrived late faced severe losses.

## Full explanation

### The altcoin universe

The broad categories of altcoins include:

- **Layer 1 blockchains** — alternative networks to Ethereum (Solana, Avalanche, Cardano, Sui). Compete on speed, cost, and developer ecosystems.
- **Layer 2 networks** — scaling solutions on top of Ethereum (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base). Often have their own native tokens.
- **DeFi tokens** — governance tokens for lending, exchange, or yield protocols (AAVE, UNI, CRV). Value often tied to protocol fee revenue or voting rights.
- **Staking/utility tokens** — tokens that pay fees, secure a network, or grant access to services.
- **Memecoins** — tokens with no technical function, driven entirely by community, humor, or celebrity attention (Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, PEPE). Can produce extreme moves in both directions.
- **Sector tokens** — tokens associated with specific themes: AI, real-world assets (RWA), gaming, privacy, data storage.

### How narratives drive prices

Crypto is unusual among financial markets because **retail attention moves price significantly**, especially in smaller assets. A narrative that gains traction on social media, at conferences, or through influential accounts can drive rapid capital inflows with little or no fundamental change in the underlying project. The sequence typically looks like:

1. An early thesis forms (often among developers or researchers)
2. Early adopters accumulate quietly
3. A catalyst (product launch, partnership, listing) triggers public attention
4. Retail capital floods in; price accelerates
5. Early holders distribute into demand
6. Attention moves to the next narrative; price collapses

### Bitcoin dominance as a rotation signal

**Bitcoin dominance** (Bitcoin's share of total crypto market cap) is a commonly watched metric for rotation:

- Rising dominance: capital flowing from riskier altcoins into Bitcoin — risk-off within crypto
- Falling dominance: capital spreading into altcoins — risk-on, often called "alt season"

The relationship is not precise and can be distorted by stablecoin flows, but the broad directional signal has been historically useful.

### Evaluating altcoins: questions that matter

Before trading an altcoin on a narrative, experienced traders ask:

- **Tokenomics** — what is the total supply? What percentage is held by the team and investors, and when does it unlock (vesting schedule)? Large upcoming unlocks are a known supply-side headwind.
- **TVL and usage** — does the protocol have actual users and locked value, or is it a token without a product?
- **Developer activity** — is the codebase actively maintained? GitHub commit history is public.
- **Liquidity** — can you exit a meaningful position without moving the market?
- **Narrative timing** — are you early, mid-cycle, or arriving after the smart money has already distributed?

### The survivorship problem

Of the thousands of altcoins launched during each bull cycle, the vast majority lose 90–99% of their value in the subsequent bear market and never recover. Evaluating altcoins requires distinguishing between genuine innovation with durable demand and speculative tokens that exist only to be distributed from early holders to late retail buyers.

### Why it matters for a trader

Altcoins are the highest-risk, highest-potential-return segment of crypto. Understanding narrative dynamics, tokenomics, and rotation patterns allows you to frame entries and exits around market structure rather than pure speculation. The question is not just "is this project good?" but "where in the narrative cycle is this trade?"
