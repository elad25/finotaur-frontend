## In plain words

**Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)** is a specific Fibonacci retracement zone — between **62% and 79%** of a price swing — that ICT identifies as the area where institutional traders most commonly re-enter in the direction of a recent impulse move. After a strong directional move, price typically pulls back before continuing. The OTE zone is where that pullback tends to pause and reverse, offering a tighter stop-loss and a better risk/reward entry than chasing the original impulse.

## Quick demo

GBP/USD makes an impulsive move from 1.2600 to 1.2800 — a 200-pip rally. Price then begins to pull back. The 62% retracement of that swing sits at 1.2676 and the 79% level sits at 1.2642. An ICT trader watches for price to enter the 1.2642–1.2676 zone, show a reaction (such as a bullish order block or a fair value gap fill in that range), and then places a long with a stop just below the 1.2600 swing low. If price rallies back toward 1.2800 or beyond, the risk/reward is approximately 3:1 or better from the OTE entry.

## Full explanation

### The Fibonacci levels used in OTE

The OTE zone is defined by three specific Fibonacci retracement levels:

- **61.8% (0.618)** — the classic "golden ratio" retracement, the most widely watched Fibonacci level across technical analysis.
- **70.5% (0.705)** — a less commonly published level that ICT emphasizes as particularly significant within the OTE zone. It represents the midpoint between 0.618 and 0.786.
- **78.6% (0.786)** — the square root of 0.618, another standard Fibonacci extension level. ICT uses 79% as the outer boundary.

The OTE entry is typically sought between 0.62 and 0.79, with the center of the zone (around 0.70–0.705) being the highest-probability target.

### How to draw the OTE

The Fibonacci tool is applied from the **origin of the impulse move** to the **end of the impulse move**:

- For a bullish OTE: drag the Fibonacci from the swing low (0%) to the swing high (100%). The retracement toward 62–79% is the OTE zone for long entries.
- For a bearish OTE: drag the Fibonacci from the swing high (0%) to the swing low (100%). The retracement back up toward 62–79% is the OTE zone for short entries.

The impulse move used should be significant — ideally, one that broke structure or swept a liquidity level — rather than a minor fluctuation.

### Requirements for a valid OTE setup

ICT practitioners do not enter every retracement into the 62–79% zone. A valid OTE setup requires additional confluence:

1. **Higher-timeframe alignment.** The OTE should be in the direction of the prevailing institutional trend (higher highs and higher lows for longs; lower highs and lower lows for shorts).
2. **Liquidity context.** The impulse move should have swept a liquidity pool (stop hunt) or broken a key structure level, confirming that institutions initiated the move.
3. **Confluent level within the zone.** An order block or fair value gap within the 62–79% range significantly improves the setup's reliability.
4. **Session timing.** Entries during the London or New York kill zones (high institutional participation) are preferred over off-hours entries.

### OTE vs. simple Fibonacci retracement

Classical technical analysis uses Fibonacci retracements (typically 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%) as potential support or resistance levels. The OTE is a narrower and more specific application: it focuses on the 62–79% zone as an *institutional re-entry area* rather than a generic support level, and it requires the additional ICT context (liquidity sweeps, order blocks, structure) to be meaningful. Used without that context, the 62–79% zone is not materially different from any other Fibonacci level.

### Practical entry mechanics

Within the OTE zone, traders typically:
- Place a limit order at the 70.5% level as the primary entry.
- Set a stop-loss just below the 79% level (for longs) or just above it (for shorts), or alternatively below the swing low of the impulse.
- Target the previous high (for longs) or previous low (for shorts) as the first profit level, with extensions to 127.2% or 161.8% as secondary targets.

### Why it matters for a trader

The OTE gives ICT practitioners a precise, pre-planned entry zone rather than a reactive chase entry. Entering at 70% of a swing with a stop at 80% means risking 10 units to potentially gain 30–50 units to the prior swing high. That structural edge in risk/reward — combined with the discipline to wait for price to come to you rather than chasing it — is one of the core practical benefits of incorporating OTE into a trading process.
