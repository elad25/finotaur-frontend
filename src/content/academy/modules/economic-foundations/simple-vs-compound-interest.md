## In plain words

**Interest** is the cost of borrowing money — or the reward for lending it. **Simple interest** is calculated only on the original amount (the **principal**). **Compound interest** is calculated on the principal plus all the interest already earned, so your interest starts earning interest. That difference sounds small but over time it is enormous.

## Quick demo

You invest $10,000 at 8% per year for 30 years.

- With **simple interest**: you earn $800 every year (8% of $10,000), so after 30 years you have $10,000 + ($800 × 30) = **$34,000**.
- With **compound interest** (annual compounding): after 30 years you have $10,000 × (1.08)^30 = **$100,627**.

Same rate. Same starting amount. Same time. Almost three times the outcome — because compound interest reinvests every year's gains.

## Full explanation

### Simple interest formula

> **Interest = Principal × Rate × Time**

If you lend a friend $1,000 at 5% simple interest for 3 years, they owe you:
$1,000 × 0.05 × 3 = $150 in interest, so $1,150 total.

Simple interest is mostly used for short-term loans, car financing, and some bonds. The math is transparent and easy to audit.

### Compound interest formula

> **A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n × t)**

Where:
- **P** = principal
- **r** = annual rate (as a decimal)
- **n** = compounding periods per year
- **t** = years

The more frequently interest compounds, the faster it grows. Daily compounding slightly outperforms monthly, which outperforms annual.

### The Rule of 72

A quick mental shortcut: divide 72 by the annual interest rate to estimate how many years it takes to double your money.

- At 6%: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
- At 9%: 72 ÷ 9 = 8 years to double
- At 12%: 72 ÷ 12 = 6 years to double

This works in reverse too: at 3% inflation, the purchasing power of cash halves in 24 years.

### The compounding frequency trap

Many financial products advertise an "annual rate" but compound more (or less) often than you expect. A credit card at 20% APR compounding monthly has an effective annual rate of about **21.9%**. Always check the **APY** (Annual Percentage Yield), which reflects actual compounding.

### Compounding works against you too

The same math that grows your savings also grows your debt. A $5,000 credit-card balance at 22% APR, paid only minimums, can take 15+ years to clear and cost more than $10,000 in total interest. Understanding compound interest is therefore as much about avoiding the wrong end of it as capturing the right end.

### Why it matters for a trader or investor

Compounding is the foundational argument for long-term investing and the mathematical enemy of constant trading costs. Every dollar paid in commissions, spreads, or bad fills is a dollar removed from the compounding base. Similarly, understanding how interest compounds drives bond pricing, option theta (time decay is compound erosion), and the cost of carrying leveraged positions overnight. The trader who internalizes compounding thinks differently about every friction cost in their strategy.
