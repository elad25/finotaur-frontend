## In plain words

A **stock screener** is a filter tool that searches the universe of thousands of publicly listed companies and returns only those that meet your specific criteria. Instead of reading about companies one by one, you define the rules — **market cap above $500M, revenue growth above 20%, P/E below 30** — and the screener shows you every stock that qualifies. Screening is how professional investors and active traders build a focused watchlist from a chaotic market.

## Quick demo

A swing trader looking for momentum setups might screen for: market cap between $1B and $20B, average daily volume above 500,000 shares, price above the 50-day moving average, and EPS growth above 15% year-over-year. On any given day that filter might return 40 names from a universe of 5,000. The trader then reviews charts on those 40 — not 5,000 — focusing effort where the quantitative criteria already confirm both quality and momentum alignment.

## Full explanation

### Why screening matters

The US alone lists roughly 5,000 to 6,000 stocks. Without a systematic filter, stock picking degrades into noise — reacting to headlines, tips, or whatever is trending on social media. Screening imposes discipline: you define what you're looking for *before* you look, which prevents the bias of working backward from a stock you already like.

### Common screening criteria

**Fundamental filters**
- Revenue and EPS growth (trailing twelve months or year-over-year)
- P/E ratio, P/S ratio, EV/EBITDA
- Profit margins and return on equity
- Debt-to-equity ratio
- Free cash flow yield

**Technical/price filters**
- Price relative to 20/50/200-day moving averages
- Relative strength vs. the S&P 500 (RS Rating)
- Average daily dollar volume (price × volume)
- 52-week high proximity
- Percentage change over N days or weeks

**Structural filters**
- Market cap range
- Sector and industry
- Exchange (NYSE, Nasdaq)
- Minimum float
- Analyst rating or earnings surprise history

### Popular screeners

Free screeners: Finviz, TradingView, Yahoo Finance Screener.
Professional-grade: Bloomberg Terminal, FactSet, Stock Rover, Koyfin.

Each platform has different data freshness and filter depth. Finviz is beginner-friendly; Bloomberg is the institutional standard.

### Building a screener strategy

A good screen is anchored to your trading style:

- **Growth trader**: high revenue growth + earnings acceleration + price near 52-week high.
- **Value investor**: low P/E + strong free cash flow + below book value.
- **Momentum trader**: strong 3-month relative performance + increasing volume + new highs.
- **Dividend investor**: consecutive dividend growth years + payout ratio below 60% + yield above 2%.

The screen is not a buy signal — it is a shortlist. Every result still requires human judgment on chart structure, business quality, and current catalyst.

### Why this matters for a trader

Without screening, most retail traders end up in overcrowded trades — the same handful of names everyone is watching. A well-constructed screen surfaces opportunities before they are mainstream, cuts your research time by 90%, and ensures every stock on your watchlist earned its place by meeting real quantitative criteria rather than noise.
