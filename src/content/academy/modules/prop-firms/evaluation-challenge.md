## In plain words

The **evaluation** (also called a challenge or audition) is the gated process a prop firm uses to identify traders who can manage risk consistently before giving them access to firm capital. You pay a fee, receive a practice account, and must hit a profit target while staying within defined loss limits — usually over a set number of trading days. Pass, and you advance to a funded account. Fail, and you start over (paying the fee again or using a reset option).

## Quick demo

A firm offers a $50,000 challenge for a $299 fee. The rules: hit an 8% profit target ($4,000) within 30 calendar days, never lose more than 5% overall ($2,500), and never lose more than 2.5% in a single day ($1,250). You have at least 5 trading days. If you reach $4,000 profit before any limit is breached, you pass and receive a funded account with an 80% profit split on real trading.

## Full explanation

### Why the evaluation exists

The evaluation does two things simultaneously for the firm:

1. **Revenue** — fees are collected from every applicant, whether they pass or not.
2. **Filtering** — it screens out traders who cannot control risk under real psychological pressure (even if the capital is simulated, the fee paid creates genuine stakes).

### Typical evaluation structure

Most evaluations share a common set of elements, though specific numbers vary significantly by firm:

- **Account size** — the notional capital you are evaluated on, commonly $10k to $200k.
- **Profit target** — a percentage you must reach to pass, typically 8–10% for a single-phase challenge.
- **Maximum drawdown** — the maximum total loss allowed from the starting balance (or the highest balance reached, depending on the firm's definition — this distinction matters).
- **Daily loss limit** — the maximum loss permitted in a single trading day, typically 4–5% of the starting balance.
- **Minimum trading days** — a requirement to trade for at least a specified number of days, preventing someone from gambling on one large position.
- **Time limit** — the maximum number of calendar or trading days to complete the challenge.

### One-phase vs. two-phase challenges

Many firms run a **two-phase** process:
- **Phase 1** — a longer phase with a higher profit target (e.g., 8%) designed to demonstrate consistent skill.
- **Phase 2** — a shorter verification phase with a lower target (e.g., 5%) that confirms the Phase 1 result was not luck.

**One-phase** challenges (or "instant funding") skip one or both phases for a higher fee and typically come with tighter ongoing rules.

### What "passing" actually means

Passing the evaluation earns you a funded account — but the funded phase is not a replica of the evaluation. Many firms impose slightly different rules in the funded phase, such as a stricter trailing drawdown or different payout schedules. Reading the full terms of both phases before paying the evaluation fee is essential.

### Why it matters for a trader

The evaluation is not just a business filter — it is the most psychologically demanding part of prop trading for most traders. Knowing both the target and the limit creates a dual pressure that does not exist when trading freely: you must win enough to qualify, but a single bad day or an overly aggressive session can end the attempt entirely. Treating the evaluation exactly as you would treat a funded account — with the same position sizing and the same rules — is the most reliable path through it.
