## In plain words

A **proprietary trading firm** (prop firm) gives a trader access to the firm's capital in exchange for a share of any profits earned. You trade with their money, take on none of your own capital risk beyond a small evaluation fee, and keep a pre-agreed cut — often 70–90% — of what you make. The firm takes the remaining slice as its return on the capital deployed.

## Quick demo

Imagine you pass a $100,000 challenge. The firm funds you with a $100,000 account. You trade it well and generate $5,000 in profit over the month. Under an 80/20 split, you receive $4,000 and the firm keeps $1,000. You never personally risked $100,000 — only the challenge fee you paid upfront to enter the evaluation.

## Full explanation

### What "prop" means

The word *proprietary* means the capital belongs to the firm, not to outside investors. Traditional prop firms like those on Wall Street hired full-time traders to sit in their offices and trade the firm's own money. The modern retail prop-firm model evolved from this: a firm creates a structured evaluation, any trader in the world can attempt it, and those who prove they can manage risk get access to real (or simulated) capital.

### The core structure

| Element | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Funded account** | The dollar amount the firm lets you trade |
| **Profit split** | Your share of profits (commonly 70–90%) |
| **Evaluation fee** | What you pay to attempt the challenge |
| **Rules** | Drawdown limits, daily loss caps, and profit targets |

### Two broad models

- **Evaluation / challenge model** — you pay a fee and must prove consistent, rule-compliant trading over a set period before being funded. Most common.
- **Instant funding** — you pay a higher fee and receive immediate access to capital, often with slightly tighter ongoing rules. A newer and faster-growing model.

### What the firm actually funds

This varies by firm and is worth understanding before signing up. Some firms trade entirely with simulated accounts and pay profits from their fee revenue (a "synthetic" model). Others place real trades through a prime broker. The distinction matters for withdrawal reliability and regulatory exposure — research any firm before paying.

### Why it matters for a trader

A prop firm is potentially a way to trade meaningful size without putting your own savings at risk. But it is not free money: the rules are strict, the evaluation fee is real money you can lose, and the ongoing funded phase has conditions that can result in account termination. Understanding the structure — before you commit a fee — is the starting point for using prop firms intelligently.
