## In plain words

Once you are in a funded account, the money you generate is split between you and the firm according to a pre-agreed **profit split** — commonly 70% to 90% in your favor. Payouts are not automatic: they happen on a schedule (often monthly, sometimes bi-weekly) and require a minimum profit threshold and sometimes a minimum number of trading days. The firm processes the payment after verifying you are within all rules.

## Quick demo

Your funded $100,000 account earns $6,000 in a month. The firm's split is 80/20. You submit a payout request after the 30-day period and the minimum withdrawal threshold of $500 is met. The firm processes $4,800 to your account (80% of $6,000). The remaining $1,200 stays with the firm. Processing typically takes 1–5 business days depending on the payment method chosen.

## Full explanation

### The profit split percentage

The advertised split is one of the most prominent numbers in prop-firm marketing, and it varies:

- **70/30** — less common today, seen in older or lower-cost firms.
- **80/20** — a widely used standard.
- **90/10** — offered by some firms, especially at higher account tiers or as a premium feature.
- **100/0** — marketed by some firms, though these accounts typically have higher fees or stricter rules that offset the apparent generosity.

Higher splits are not always better if they come with harder rules, lower account sizes, or less reliable payout processes. The net amount received matters more than the percentage headline.

### Payout schedules and conditions

Most firms do not allow daily withdrawals. Common structures include:

- **Monthly payouts** — the most common. A profit threshold (e.g., $500 minimum) must be reached before a withdrawal is permitted.
- **Bi-weekly payouts** — offered by some firms as a premium or at higher account tiers.
- **On-demand payouts** — rarer; some firms allow withdrawals at any time above a minimum amount.

Conditions attached to payouts vary. Some firms require a trader to:
- Have been funded for a minimum number of days before the first payout.
- Not be in an open drawdown (account must be profitable at time of request).
- Maintain a minimum balance after the withdrawal.

### What "profit" means in the split

Not all firms define profit the same way. Most calculate the split on **net profit above the original account starting balance** — so fees, commissions, and swap costs are typically already subtracted before the split. Confirm with each firm exactly what the base for the split calculation is.

### Payment methods and timing

Common payout methods include:
- Bank wire (SWIFT/SEPA)
- Cryptocurrency (USDT, BTC)
- Rise or Deel (payment platforms popular in the prop space)
- PayPal or Wise (less common)

Processing times vary from same-day to 5 business days. International bank wires can add additional delays. Always confirm a firm's payout reliability through independent reviews before committing.

### Why it matters for a trader

The payout mechanics determine whether a prop firm is operationally viable for a given trader. A firm with an excellent split percentage but a 45-day wait for first payouts and inconsistent processing creates cash-flow problems. Before selecting a firm, map out what the actual monthly income would look like after the split — and whether the payout schedule and minimum thresholds match the trading frequency and profit velocity of your strategy.
